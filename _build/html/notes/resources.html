Using BLM Navigator¶

After going to BLM Naviagtor, then do -> Toolbox -> PLSS Tools -> Add ‘PLSS CadNSDI Layer’ to Map. Also select Get Lat/Long from Land Description. Next, choose State of Indina, 2nd Meridian, the Township number, Range number, and section number. Then click ‘Find Land Description from Location’, and it will take you to that section, township and range.

Note

I am not sure how to Aliquot box should be filled in.

BLM Land Catalog allows you to choose State, Township and Range, and it will display it and all numbered sections of the townships.

The U.S. Geological Survey websit’s article The Public Land Survey System (PLSS) gives illustrations and explain the meaning of the numbers in the table. It explains:

Each township is identified with a township and range designation. Township designations indicate the location north or south of the baseline, and range designations indicate the location east or west of the Principal Meridian. For example, a township might be identified as Township 7 North, Range 2 West, which would mean that it was in the 7th tier of townships north of a baseline, and in the 2nd column of townships west of a principal meridian. A legal land description of a section includes the State, Principal Meridian name, Township and Range designations with directions, and the section number: Nebraska, Sixth Principal Meridian T7N, R2W, sec5.

How Civil War Draft worked in Indiana¶

The article Conscription and Draft in Indiana During the Civil War explains how the process of the Civil War draft, both the initial registration and the subsequent draft of some as new soldiers into the Army of the United States, was implemented at the county and township level in the state of Indiana.
